Introduction

The Swiss Confederation was founded in 1291 as a defensive alliance among three cantons but was expanded when localities started to join.

Switzerland was not involved in either of the two World Wars, what makes it one of the most neutral countries in Europe.

Nowadays, Switzerland remains active in many United Nations and international organizations but retains a strong commitment to neutrality.

However, the political and economic integration of Europe over the past half century, has strengthened Switzerland's ties with its neighbors.

People and Society

Population and Ethnic groups

Switzerland has about 8,563,760 people (2023 est.) making it the 102nd most populated country in the world.

Its ethnic groups are the following: Swiss 69.2%, German 4.2%, Italian 3.2%, Portuguese 2.5%, French 2.1%, Kosovan 1.1%, Turkish 1%, other 16.7% (2020 est.)

Languages

Switzerland has four official languages, those being Swiss German (62.1%), French (22.8%), Italian (8%) and Romansh (0.5%).

However, other languages like English (5.7%), Portuguese (3.5%), Spanish (2.3%) are also spoken.

Economy

Economic Overview

Switzerland is not part of the European Union economy, but that does not stop the state to be a high-income country.

It is a renowned banking and financial hub, with extremely low unemployment and highly skilled workforce

The country is also a key pharmaceutical and precision manufacturing exporter, but with a considerable high public debt
